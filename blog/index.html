A Longer and More Interesting Title Title

June 11, 2017

Disney Marathon is the perfect race for first time marathoner. Cruising through the most magical place on earth is certainly an effective way to take your mind off the miles, and onto fun distractions and attractions. Running up to Cinderella’s castle was exhilarating and if your pace is relaxed enough, you can even take a quick break to ride Expedition Everest when you hit Animal Kingdom.
But, if you've never runDisney in Kissimmee Florida before, I'll let you in on a secret. The Walt Disney World is a BIG place. Long expanses of roads connect the small patches of park scattered throughout the property. You will spend more time outside the parks than in them. It's flat, making for a beginner friendly course, but it's also ugly. This isn't a huge pitfall because just like waiting in line for a park ride, entertainment lines the streets from start to finish including characters, live animals, and DJs. I signed up for this race only a few months prior to race weekend, and I didn’t even follow through with my last minute training. One 15 miler was my longest practice run. Yikes! Despite this horrible idea to run a marathon untrained (yes, I got injured), I was still able to pound out those last ten miles with a huge smile on my face, because the course was filled with entertainment and motivational supporters with neon signs overflowing with puns and positivity. This is what I learned from my first Disney Marathon Weekend.
