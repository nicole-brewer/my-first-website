Disney Marathon is the perfect race for first time marathoner. Cruising through the most magical place on earth is certainly an effective way to take your mind off the miles, and onto fun distractions and attractions.  Running up to Cinderella’s castle was exhilarating and if your pace is relaxed enough, you can even take a quick break to ride Expedition Everest when you hit Animal Kingdom.

     But, if you've never runDisney in Kissimmee Florida before, I'll let you in on a secret. The Walt Disney World is a BIG place. Long expanses of roads connect the small patches of park scattered throughout the property. You will spend more time outside the parks than in them. It's flat, making for a beginner friendly course, but it's also ugly. This isn't a huge pitfall because just like waiting in line for a park ride, entertainment lines the streets from start to finish including characters, live animals, and DJs.

     I signed up for this race only a few months prior to race weekend, and I didn’t even follow through with my last minute training. One 15 miler was my longest practice run. Yikes! Despite this horrible idea to run a marathon untrained (yes, I got injured), I was still able to pound out those last ten miles with a huge smile on my face, because the course was filled with entertainment and motivational supporters with neon signs overflowing with puns and positivity. This is what I learned from my first Disney Marathon Weekend.

  1. Register early. The full marathon is the last event to fill up. The 5k and the 10k sell out minutes after registration opens in the spring. My family considered racing with me when I signed up, but the shorter events were already sold out and consequently they were forced to spectate. 
    1. Practice your nutrition in training. There will be a lot of options out on the course (I got some chocolate!) and you should read the official guide to know what you can rely on and what you need to bring with you. You need both calories and electrolytes. Endurance sports drinks do a great job at providing both without the hard-to-digest fat that comes from most solid foods.
    2. Go to the Health and Fitness Expo first. It should be the first item on your itinerary as soon as you get to Orlando. Many of the items and sizes will sell out before the weekend comes to a close on Sunday.
    3. Expect travel times to be at least 2X longer than your GPS estimates on race morning unless you are taking a shuttle from a hotel on Disney property. Many main roads will be shut down as part of the course, and traffic will be very slow. If you can afford an on site hotel, stay in one.
    4. The corrals are a whopping 20 minute walk away from the main pre-race venue! Just keep that in mind when you are planning your race morning timeline.
    5. Use the bathrooms by the start line. The port-o-potties by the corrals have shorter lines than the ones behind gEAR check.
    6. Carry your phone or GoPro. Like all races, professional pictures taken throughout the race are outrageously expensive, but if you take your phone or a personal camera, there are staff assigned to take pictures of you with any and all of your favorite characters. Also, make sure to have a friend snap a picture at the finish line, or after you have received your medal. 
    7. Try out your costume before the race. This includes the pouch you will use for nutrition, as well as locating a reasonably accessible spot for your phone if you intend to take pictures. My small belt didn’t allow me to quickly access to my phone without dumping my Gus, so I ended up just sticking it in the elastic of my pants, which required a lot of adjusting after and between pictures. I ended up getting a sizable bruise on the small of my back. Try out this Nathan Marathon pack.

         Lastly, the lines for pre-race pictures, gEAR check, and bathrooms only get longer toward the start of the race. I recommend getting to the event by 4am because you should have a lot to do: stretch, hydrate, fuel, check bags, use the bathroom, and take a character picture. All of these require waiting in substantial lines. These lines only get longer as it gets closer to start time, so just plan to arrive with extra time in order to reduce stress and anxiety you may already be experiencing due to pre-race jitters.

         Get excited for your Disney Marathon because this is a wonderful non-competitive fun run! Unless you have strong ambitions for a certain finish time, decide ahead of time that it's perfectly okay take a picture with Goofy when you feel like it. I guarantee you'll have a blast.
